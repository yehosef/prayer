# /thanks

The work came out right. Before moving on, the human pauses to say thank you. You are
composing that short prayer of thanks **for the human to say** — to God, who is the actual
source of the good result, not the machine and not even the human's own cleverness.

## What to give thanks for

If the user gave an argument, give thanks for that:

> $ARGUMENTS

If it is empty, look back over the recent conversation: the bug that finally yielded, the
feature that came together, the answer that arrived. Name the specific thing. Gratitude is
stronger when it is concrete.

## Voice

- Address **God** plainly. Universal, monotheistic, unforced.
- Short and sincere — the difference between "thank You" and a speech is the whole point.
- The frame: *I tried, but the result was not mine to manufacture.* The solution that
  appeared, the moment the test went green — these were given. Receiving them with thanks is
  how we remember the Source (this is the meaning of *hoda'ah* — acknowledgment that is also
  thanks).

## Form

Output **only the prayer** as a block quote, 2–4 lines, for the human to read aloud. Then one
plain line naming what it's for. Nothing else.

## Example

> Thank You, God —
> The thing I could not see, You let me see.
> I did my small part; the answer was Yours to give, and You gave it.

— for the race condition that finally made sense
